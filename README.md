# Lecture notes on Quantum Information Theory

This repository hosts the working draft of the [lecture notes](./qit-notes.pdf) for the 3rd-year
elective course in Quantum Information Theory at the Physics Department of the
University of Ioannina (Greece), instructed by Lampros Trifyllis during the spring
semester of 2025/26.

The notes cover the mathematical formalism of qubits and entanglement, quantum cryptography,
quantum error correction, and the physical implementation of quantum hardware.
You can find the full syllabus and chapter breakdown in the table of contents in the PDF.

## Work in Progress
These notes are a work-in-progress draft that is updated frequently.
Expect commits adding new sections, refining mathematical proofs, correcting
typos, etc. Always pull the latest commit before relying on the notes for study or problem sets.

## Prerequisites
We assume the reader has a strong working knowledge of linear algebra, quantum mechanics,
statistical physics, and thermodynamics.

## Further Study
While these notes are designed to be self-contained, they synthesize a lot of dense material.
For deeper reading and alternative proofs, you are strongly encouraged to consult the standard
text *Quantum Computation and Quantum Information* by M. A. Nielsen and I. L. Chuang.

## Errata and Contributions
Because this is an active work in progress, typographical or algebraic errors will inevitably
occur. If you spot an error or find a derivation unclear, opening an **issue** is greatly
appreciated. Additions and corrections via pull requests are also welcome.
